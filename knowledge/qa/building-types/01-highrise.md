# High-Rise Buildings (>30m)

## Definition and Key Thresholds

- **High-rise**: Building exceeding **30 metres** in height
- All floors must be compartment floors above 30m
- Compartment floors required above **9 metres** for buildings under 30m
- Fire lifts required above **4 storeys** or **18 metres**

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII

## Refuge Floors

- Temporary shelter levels during fire emergencies
- Required every **20-30 floors** (international benchmark, varies by jurisdiction)
- Must be accessible from all staircases, equipped with fire safety facilities
- Functions: safe haven for evacuees and disabled persons, firefighter command points

> **Citation**: MS 1183:2015 Annex G

## Fire Lifts and Pressurized Staircases

- Fire lift: protected shaft, **2-hour** lobby fire resistance, minimum car **1.1m x 2.1m**
- Pressurized staircases: mechanical ventilation for buildings over **18 metres**
- Fire command centre at ground floor for large high-rise buildings

## By-Law 124: Lift Requirements

- Lift shafts enclosed within fire-rated construction
- Emergency mode during mains power failure
- Fire mode for firefighter use
- Shaft ventilation and smoke detectors for lobbies
- **By-Law 224A** (2021): Hospital fire escape bed lift provision

> **Citation**: UBBL By-Law 124; By-Law 224A (Amendment 2021)
