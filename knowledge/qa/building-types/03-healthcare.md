# Healthcare Facilities (Hospital/Clinic)

## Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 1998 (Act 586)

### Approval Process

- Private hospitals require **Director General of Health** approval before establishment
- Application for operating licence must be made within **3 years** from approval date
- Private clinics require registration with a certificate of registration from the Director General
- Practitioner must be registered with **Malaysian Medical Council (MMC)** with an Annual Practicing Certificate (APC)

> **Citation**: Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 1998 (Act 586)

### Facilities Covered

- Private hospital, psychiatric hospital, ambulatory care centre
- Nursing home, maternity home, blood bank
- Haemodialysis centre, hospice, community mental health centre

### Building Requirements

- Detailed floor plans required during registration
- Premises inspection by **CKAPS** (Malaysian Private Medical Practice Control Section)
- Accreditation from **MSQH** (Malaysian Society for Quality in Health) recommended

## Hospital Fire Safety (UBBL)

- Purpose Group II (Institutional) under UBBL
- Patient wards: compartment with minimum **1-hour fire resistance**
- Door openings: minimum **half-hour fire resistance** doors
- Automatic fire alarm system mandatory

> **Citation**: UBBL Part VII (Purpose Group II)

## By-Law 224A: Hospital Fire Escape — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

By-Law 224A is a comprehensive new by-law with **9 subsections** covering fire safety for hospitals:

### (1) No Beds in Basement
- Patient beds shall **not** be located in any basement level
- Basements may only be used for ancillary services (parking, storage, plant rooms)

### (2) Minimum Exit Width
- Exit doors and corridors serving patient areas: minimum **1.2 metres** wide (to accommodate hospital beds)
- All doors on escape routes must be wide enough for bed evacuation

### (3) Fire Escape Bed Lift
- Fire escape **bed lift** required for all hospitals with patient beds above or below ground floor
- Bed lift minimum internal dimensions: **2.7 metres deep × 1.8 metres wide** (to accommodate bed + attendants)
- No protected lobby needed if no unprotected opening within **3 metres** horizontally of the lift entrance
- Emergency power supply required for bed lifts

### (4) Fire Shelters / Refuge Areas
- Minimum **2 fire shelters** per floor in patient ward areas
- Each shelter must accommodate **≥50% of the beds** on that floor
- Fire shelters must be separated from the ward by compartment walls with fire resistance equal to the building's structural requirement
- Direct access to a protected staircase from each shelter

### (5) Corridor Ventilation
- Corridors serving patient areas must have natural ventilation of **≥15%** of the corridor floor area
- Where natural ventilation is not achievable, smoke extract/pressurization system required

### (6) Smoke Chamber
- Smoke chamber minimum area: **≥6 sq metres**
- Located at each staircase landing serving patient floors
- Provides buffer zone between ward and protected staircase

### (7) Operating Theatre and ICU
- Operating theatres and ICUs must be in separate fire compartments
- Fire resistance of enclosing walls: minimum **2 hours**
- Independent smoke extract system required

### (8) Outpatient Exemption
- Outpatient clinics and diagnostic areas (where patients are ambulatory) are exempt from bed lift and shelter requirements
- Standard escape provisions (By-Laws 165–194) apply instead

### (9) Fire Alarm and Detection
- Hospitals must have a fire alarm system with **zone mapping** displayed at the fire command centre
- Alarm may be **two-stage** (staff alert first, general alarm second) to allow managed evacuation
- All patient areas must have smoke detectors — heat detectors alone are insufficient

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 224A
