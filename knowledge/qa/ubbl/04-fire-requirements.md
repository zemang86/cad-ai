# Part VII — Fire Requirements

## Purpose Groups (By-Law 134 / Schedule 5)

| Group | Designation | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| I | Small Residential | Houses, flats (up to 4 storeys) |
| II | Institutional | Hospitals, care homes |
| III | Other Residential | Hotels, hostels, boarding houses |
| IV | Office | Office buildings |
| V | Shop | Retail premises |
| VI | Factory | Manufacturing, workshops |
| VII | Place of Assembly | Theatres, halls, mosques |
| VIII | Storage and General | Warehouses, car parks |

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Law 134

## By-Law 133: Fire Resistance Requirements

| Purpose Group | Height <= 18m | Height > 18m |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| I (Small residential, <=3 storeys) | **30 minutes** | N/A |
| I (Small residential, >3 storeys) | **60 minutes** | N/A |
| II-VII (All other groups) | **1 hour** | **2 hours** |

- Elements of structure (columns, beams, floors, load-bearing walls) shall have the specified fire resistance
- Basements: minimum **2 hours** fire resistance regardless of building height
- Buildings over **30 metres**: ALL floors must be compartment floors
- Buildings under 30 metres: compartment floors required above **9 metres**

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Law 133, Schedule 9

## By-Law 136: Compartmentation (Schedule 5)

Compartment size limits without sprinklers:

| Purpose Group | Height | Max Floor Area | Max Volume |
|--------------|--------|---------------|------------|
| III (Hotel) | > 28m | **2,000 sq m** | **5,500 cu m** |
| IV (Office) | > 28m | No limit | No limit |
| V (Shop) | All | **2,000 sq m** | **7,000 cu m** |
| VI (Factory) | > 28m | **2,000 sq m** | **5,500 cu m** |
| VIII (Storage) | <= 28m | No limit | **21,000 cu m** |
| VIII (Storage) | > 28m | **1,000 sq m** | No limit |

- With automatic sprinkler system: compartment size limits are **doubled (x2)** (By-Law 136)
- Compartment walls shall have fire resistance not less than the structural elements

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Law 136, Schedule 5

## By-Law 136a: Fire Shutters — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

- Fire shutters may be used in lieu of fire doors for openings in compartment walls/floors where doors are impractical
- Must comply with **PM 1073** (Malaysian Standard for fire shutters)
- **Activation methods**: automatic (fusible link or smoke/heat detector), manual release at both sides
- Thermal insulation: fire shutter must provide insulation equivalent to the fire resistance period of the wall it protects
- **Signage**: "FIRE SHUTTER — DO NOT OBSTRUCT" clearly displayed on both sides
- Testing and maintenance: annual inspection and testing required, records kept for JBPM inspection
- 7-paragraph by-law covering specifications, installation, activation, signage, maintenance, testing, and enforcement

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 136a

## By-Law 138a: Partition Walls — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

- Partition walls separating different occupancies or fire compartments must comply with the **Ninth Schedule** fire resistance requirements
- Non-load-bearing partitions used as fire barriers must achieve the full fire resistance period specified for the compartment

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 138a

## By-Law 139: Fire Risk Area Isolation — 2021 Amendment

- Areas of high fire risk must be isolated from the rest of the building by construction having fire resistance of not less than **2 hours**
- Fire risk areas include:
  - **Boiler rooms** and heating plant rooms
  - **LPG storage** rooms and gas meter rooms
  - **Transformer rooms** and switch rooms
  - **Generator rooms**
  - **Kitchen and cooking areas** (commercial/institutional)
  - **Hospital laboratories** and pharmaceutical stores
  - **Paint stores** and chemical stores
  - **Refuse collection chambers**
  - Any room containing **flammable liquids or gases** exceeding prescribed quantities
- Doors to fire risk areas: minimum **1-hour fire resistance**, self-closing

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 139

## By-Law 140: Fire Appliance Access — 2021 Amendment (REPLACED)

The 2021 Amendment completely replaced By-Law 140 with new fire appliance access requirements:

- Access road for fire appliances: minimum **6 metres** width (clear of obstructions)
- Road must support a load of **30 tonnes** (fire engine + water)
- Maximum gradient: **1:15** for straight roads, **1:9** for turning areas
- Minimum vertical clearance: **4.5 metres**
- Turning area: minimum **15 metres** turning radius for fire appliances
- **Signage**: "NO PARKING — FIRE ACCESS" or equivalent, at regular intervals
- Fire appliance access must be maintained at all times — no permanent or temporary obstructions
- For buildings >18m: fire appliance hardstanding within **18 metres** of dry riser inlet

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 140

## By-Law 144: External Wall Coatings — 2021 Amendment

- External wall cladding within **2 metres** of the boundary must be **non-combustible**
- Cladding systems must be tested per **PB 8414** (full-scale fire test) or equivalent
- Combustible insulation behind non-combustible cladding must include fire barriers at every floor level

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 144

## Travel Distance (By-Law 165 / Schedule 7)

Maximum travel distance to nearest exit:

| Condition | Maximum Distance |
|-----------|-----------------|
| With sprinklers (general) | **45 metres** |
| Without sprinklers (general) | **30 metres** |
| Dead-end (general, <=6 persons) | **15 metres** |
| Dead-end (shop/car park, <6 persons) | **12 metres** |

Travel distance limits per Purpose Group (Schedule 7):

| Purpose Group | Dead End | With Sprinklers |
|--------------|----------|-----------------|
| III (Hotel) | **10 metres** | **45 metres** |
| IV (Office) | **15 metres** | **60 metres** |
| V (Shop) | **15 metres** | **45 metres** |
| VIII (Basement) | **15 metres** | **30 metres** |

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Law 165, Schedule 7

## By-Law 174a: Final Exit — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

- Every means of escape must lead to a **final exit** that discharges:
  - Directly to a **street or open space** at ground level, OR
  - To a **protected route** leading to a street or open space
- Final exit must be of sufficient width to accommodate the total number of persons using it
- Final exit doors must open in the direction of escape travel

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 174a

## Exit Width and Capacity (By-Laws 175-177 / Schedule 7)

Exit width calculation: **550mm** per unit of exit width.

| Building Type | Occupant Load Factor | Persons per Exit Unit |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Office | 10 sq m/person | 60 |
| Hotel | 2 pax/room | 45 |
| Shopping | 6 sq m/person | 60 |

- No exit shall be less than **1.0 metre** wide
- Minimum number of exits: **2** for any floor with occupant load > 50
- Corridor/passageway height: minimum **2.4 metres**

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Laws 175-177, Schedule 7

## By-Law 194: Single Staircase — 2021 Amendment (REPLACED)

The 2021 Amendment replaced By-Law 194:

- Single staircase permitted ONLY where:
  - Occupancy above ground is **domestic or office**, AND
  - Topmost occupied floor is **≤12 metres** above fire appliance access level
  - **OR**: Building is **≤2 storeys** with the first floor level not exceeding **6 metres** above ground
- Ground floor may be shops or car parks
- Single staircase must be a **protected staircase** with fire-rated enclosure

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 194

## By-Law 197a/197b: Fire-Fighting Holes and Access — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

### By-Law 197a: Fire-Fighting Holes
- Required for buildings exceeding **18 metres** in height
- Openings in external walls for firefighter access and ventilation during fire-fighting operations
- Must be clearly marked on external façade

### By-Law 197b: Fire-Fighting Access Space
- Each purpose group in a building must have a dedicated **fire lift** for firefighter access
- Minimum fire-fighting access space of **6 sq metres** at each floor level adjacent to the fire lift lobby
- Space must be kept clear and unobstructed at all times

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Laws 197a, 197b

## By-Law 202: Stair Pressurization — 2021 Amendment

- Pressurized/smoke-free staircases required for buildings exceeding **45 metres** in height (amended from previous >30m threshold)
- Pressurization system must maintain positive pressure differential of **50 Pa** when all doors are closed
- System must be connected to emergency power supply

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 202

## By-Law 211a: Construction Materials — 2021 Amendment (NEW)

- **Plastic materials** (including polycarbonate, acrylic, PVC) are **prohibited** for use in:
  - External and internal **wall construction**
  - **Ceiling** construction and finishes
  - **Roof** construction (except rooflights limited to 20% of roof area with fire barriers)
- Exceptions: minor components (conduits, pipes) where adequately fire-stopped

> **Citation**: UBBL (Amendment 2021), By-Law 211a

## By-Law 153: Fire Lifts

- Fire lifts required for buildings exceeding **4 storeys** or **18 metres** in height
- Fire lift lobby shall be a protected lobby with fire resistance not less than **2 hours**
- Fire lift shall have minimum car size: **1.1m x 2.1m**

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part VII, By-Law 153
