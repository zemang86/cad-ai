# General Provisions

## By-Law 2: Definitions

Key definitions under UBBL:
- **Building**: Any structure whether permanent or temporary erected on any land (UBBL By-Law 2)
- **Building operations**: Erecting, re-erecting, or making alterations to a building (UBBL By-Law 2)
- **Designated building**: Buildings which require a fire certificate under the Fire Services Act 1988 (UBBL By-Law 2)

## By-Law 3: Classification of Buildings

Buildings are classified by purpose group:

| Group | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| I | Small residential | Detached, semi-detached, terrace houses |
| II | Large residential | Flats, apartments, condominiums |
| III | Institutional | Hospitals, schools, homes for elderly |
| IV | Office | Office buildings, banks |
| V | Shop/commercial | Shops, malls, markets, restaurants |
| VI | Factory/warehouse | Factories, workshops, warehouses |
| VII | Place of assembly | Cinemas, theatres, places of worship |
| VIII | Storage/special | Car parks, petrol stations |

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part II, By-Law 3

## By-Law 5: Submission of Plans

- Plans must be submitted to the local authority for approval before commencement of building operations (UBBL By-Law 5(1))
- Plans shall be prepared by a Professional Architect or Professional Engineer registered under the relevant Acts (UBBL By-Law 5(2))
- Plans must include: site plan, floor plans, elevations, sections, structural plans (UBBL By-Law 5(3))

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part II, By-Law 5

## By-Law 16: Approved Plans

- No person shall erect any building except in accordance with approved plans (UBBL By-Law 16(1))
- Deviation from approved plans requires fresh approval from the local authority (UBBL By-Law 16(2))

> **Citation**: Uniform Building By-Laws 1984, Part II, By-Law 16
