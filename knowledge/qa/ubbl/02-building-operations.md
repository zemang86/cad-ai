# Part II — Submission of Plans for Approval

## By-Law 3: Submission of Plans for Approval

- All plans for the erection of any building shall be submitted to the local authority for approval
- Plans must be submitted by a **principal submitting person (PSP)** or **submitting person** registered under the relevant professional Act

> **Citation:** By-law 3, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 4: Return of Plan

- Local authority may return plans if beyond the competence of the submitting person
- Returned plans may be re-submitted with a certificate from the relevant competent authority certifying competence

> **Citation:** By-law 4, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 5: Supervision of Work

- No erection of a building shall take place unless the PSP or authorised person undertakes supervision
- Erection must be under **fulltime supervision** of a construction project manager assisted by a construction site supervisor (accredited by CIDB/LPIPM)
- A site supervisory staff shall be appointed to ensure compliance with safety measures, erection qualities, plans and specifications

> **Citation:** By-law 5, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-Law 6: Plans to be Signed

- All plans shall be signed by the PSP and by the owner or agent, bearing the full address of the owner
- Local authority may direct the owner to execute required work if the owner has refused or failed to do so

> **Citation:** By-law 6, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 7: Withdrawal or Change of PSP

- PSP responsible for proper execution of works until completion, unless:
  - Another PSP is appointed with local authority agreement, or
  - Local authority accepts withdrawal before erection has commenced
- Where PSP has died, become bankrupt, deregistered, or ceased to practise — owner must appoint replacement as soon as practicable

> **Citation:** By-law 7, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 8: Plans to be Deposited

- All building plans shall be deposited in the manner determined by the local authority
- One set retained by local authority; other set returned after approval
- Plans deemed deposited at the time received by the local authority
- Disapproved plans returned with statement explaining reasons

> **Citation:** By-law 8, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-Law 9: Scale of Plans

| Plan Type | Minimum Scale |
|---|---|
| Site plans | **1:1,000** |
| Key/location plans | Any convenient scale |
| General building plans | **1:100** (may be 1:200 in special cases) |
| Sketch plans for approval in principle | **1:200** |

- Plans may be drawn in International System of Units

> **Citation:** By-law 9, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 10: Plans Required

All building plans shall contain (unless inapplicable):

**(a) Site plan** showing: lot number, street access, distance from road centre, lot dimensions, surface/foul water drainage, scale/north point, clearances to boundaries, existing/proposed ground levels, SPAH elements (for bungalows/semi-D with roof ≥100 m² and all detached buildings with roof ≥100 m²)

**(b) Floor plans** showing: room dimensions, wall thickness, door/window openings, daylight areas, drain lines, room names/uses

**(c) Sections** showing: existing/proposed ground levels, street and drain levels, foundation widths/depths, wall thickness, storey heights, staircase details, joist/beam sizes, roof construction, materials

**(d) Elevations** showing: footway/road levels, adjoining building levels, proposed materials

> **Citation:** By-law 10, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2011/2021)

## By-Law 11: Exemption from By-Law 10

- Local authority may exempt from any/all requirements of By-law 10

> **Citation:** By-law 11, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 12: Sketch Plans for Approval in Principle

- Tentative sketch plans with brief report may be submitted for approval in principle
- Subject to fees in First Schedule
- After approval in principle, full plans per By-laws 3–10 and 14–16 must be submitted before construction

> **Citation:** By-law 12, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-Law 13: Special Permission to Commence Building Operations

- Local authority may grant written permission to commence building operations before full plan approval
- Such permission does not limit the local authority's power to give further directions

> **Citation:** By-law 13, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 14: Plans of Alterations

- Parts to be removed shown in **dotted lines**; new work in **red or fully coloured black**
- All existing drainage, stairs, windows, doors, and openings must be shown

> **Citation:** By-law 14, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 15: Specifications

- Local authority may require plans to be accompanied by specifications of all materials proposed

> **Citation:** By-law 15, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 16: Details and Calculations of Structural Plans

- Structural plans and calculations shall be submitted before commencement of construction
- Structural plans must bear certificate by submitting person (Form A, Second Schedule)
- All structural plans clearly marked to indicate imposed loads for each floor system

> **Citation:** By-law 16, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-Law 17: Power to Reject Structural Plans

- Local authority may examine and reject structural plans/calculations not in accordance with the By-laws
- May require re-submission of new plans for the rejected portion

> **Citation:** By-law 17, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 18: Permits

- Sketch plans may be submitted for minor erections/alterations in lieu of approved plans
- Permits may be issued for fencing encroaching on footway

> **Citation:** By-law 18, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 19: Temporary Permits

Temporary permits may be issued for:
- Shed for shows or place of worship
- Builder's working shed/store
- Depositing building materials on streets
- Erection of scaffolding on street
- Staging/framework on roof abutting street
- Hoarding on streets (By-law 20)
- Temporary buildings (subject to First Schedule conditions)

> **Citation:** By-law 19, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 20: Advertisement Hoardings

- Subject to annual temporary permit from local authority
- Plans/sketch plans to be submitted
- Certified by submitting person for structural safety

> **Citation:** By-law 20, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 21: Materials Not to be Deposited on Street

- No building materials on any street without temporary permit (By-law 19)
- Materials must be fenced/enclosed and suitably lighted during darkness
- Watchman required after working hours

> **Citation:** By-law 21, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 22: Notice of Commencement or Resumption

- **7 days' written notice** to local authority before commencement or resumption of building operations

> **Citation:** By-law 22, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 23: Notice of Completion of Setting Out

- Written notice to local authority upon completion of setting out
- Local authority has **7 days** to inspect; if no objection within 7 days, builder may proceed

> **Citation:** By-law 23, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 24: Notice of Completion of Excavation for Foundation

- Written notice upon completion of foundation excavation
- Local authority has **3 days** to inspect; foundation work may proceed if no objection within 3 days

> **Citation:** By-law 24, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 25: Certificate of Completion and Compliance (CCC)

- Upon completion, the principal submitting person (PSP) shall issue a CCC
- The CCC certifies that the building has been constructed in accordance with the approved plans
- The CCC must be accompanied by:
  - Form F (certificate by architect/engineer)
  - Form G1-G21 (certificates by specialist contractors)
  - Fire certificate (for designated buildings)

> **Citation:** By-law 25, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 1999/2007)

## By-Law 25A: Certificate of Completion and Compliance

- Additional provisions for CCC issuance under the 2007/2021 amendments

> **Citation:** By-law 25A, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007/2021)

## By-Law 26: Temporary Certificate of Fitness for Occupation

- Local authority may issue a temporary certificate permitting occupation before full completion
- Subject to conditions as the local authority deems fit

> **Citation:** By-law 26, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 27: Partial Certificate of Completion and Compliance

- A partial CCC may be issued for a completed portion of a building
- The completed portion must be independently functional and safe for occupation

> **Citation:** By-law 27, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2007)

## By-Law 28: Offences

- Penalties for contravention of Part II provisions

> **Citation:** By-law 28, UBBL 1984

## By-Law 29: Fees for Consideration of Plans and Permits

- Fees as prescribed in the **First Schedule**
- Half fees for places of religious worship, schools, or charitable purposes

> **Citation:** By-law 29, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)
