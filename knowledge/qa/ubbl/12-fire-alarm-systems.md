# Part VIII — Fire Alarm System and Fire Extinguishment System

## By-law 225: Fire Alarm System and Extinguishment System

- Fire alarm and fire extinguishment systems shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule**
- Systems must comply with relevant Malaysian Standards
- The Director General of Fire and Rescue may impose additional requirements based on building occupancy and risk

> **Citation:** By-law 225, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 226: Automatic System for Hazardous Occupancy

- Buildings with hazardous occupancy shall be provided with automatic fire detection and suppression systems
- Requirements determined by the Director General based on the nature of the hazard

> **Citation:** By-law 226, UBBL 1984

## By-law 226A: Hose Reel Systems

- Hose reel systems shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule** and **MS 1489**
- Hose reels must be located so that every part of the floor area is within reach of a hose reel jet

> **Citation:** By-law 226A, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 227: Portable Extinguishers

- Portable fire extinguishers shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule**
- Type and size of extinguisher must be appropriate to the fire risk in each area

> **Citation:** By-law 227, UBBL 1984

## By-law 228: Sprinkler Systems

- Automatic sprinkler systems shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule** and relevant Malaysian Standards
- Sprinkler systems, where installed, **double the permitted compartment size limits** (By-law 136)

> **Citation:** By-law 228, UBBL 1984

## By-law 228A: Automatic Fire Monitoring System

- An automatic fire monitoring system connected to the appropriate fire station shall be provided as required by the Tenth Schedule

> **Citation:** By-law 228A, UBBL 1984

## By-law 229: Means of Access and Fire Fighting in Buildings Over 18m

- Buildings exceeding **18 metres** in height shall provide:
  - Fire fighting access and facilities as required by the Director General
  - Dry riser or wet riser systems as applicable

> **Citation:** By-law 229, UBBL 1984

## By-law 230: Dry Riser Systems

- Dry riser systems shall be designed and installed in accordance with **MS 1489**
- Required for buildings where the topmost floor is between **18 metres and 30 metres** above fire appliance access level
- Landing valves shall be provided on every floor in the fire fighting access lobby or adjacent to the fire fighting staircase

> **Citation:** By-law 230, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 231: Wet Riser Systems

- Wet riser systems shall be designed and installed in accordance with **MS 1489**
- Required for buildings where the topmost floor exceeds **30 metres** above fire appliance access level
- Hose connection shall be provided in each fire fighting access lobby or adjacent to a fire fighting staircase on every floor
- Each stage of the wet riser shall not exceed **150 metres** in height
- Pressure for each stage shall not exceed **20 bar**
- Height between topmost and lowest landing valves in each stage shall not exceed **75 metres**
- Each stage must be provided with its own pump set

> **Citation:** By-law 231, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-law 232: Wet or Dry Riser System for Buildings Under Construction

- Where wet or dry riser is required, at least one riser shall be installed when the building under construction has reached a height above the fire brigade pumping inlet level
- Riser shall be extended as construction progresses to within **2 floors** of the topmost floor under construction
- Where designed height requires wet riser, fire pumps, water storage tanks and water main connections shall be provided

> **Citation:** By-law 232, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-law 235: Fixed Extinguishing System

- Fixed extinguishing systems shall be either:
  - **Total flooding system**
  - **Local application system**
  - **Unit protection system**
- System type depends on the nature of hazard, process and occupancy
- Systems must be approved by the Director General

> **Citation:** By-law 235, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 236: Special Hazards

- Places constituting special hazards or risk due to storage, trade, occupancy or size shall be protected by fixed installations, protective devices, systems and special extinguishers as required by the Director General

> **Citation:** By-law 236, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 237: Fire Alarm Systems

- Fire alarm systems shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule** and **MS 1745**

> **Citation:** By-law 237, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 238: Fire Command Centre

- Fire command centre shall be provided in accordance with the **Tenth Schedule**
- Location: on the fire appliance access level
- Must contain panels to monitor:
  - Public address system
  - Fireman intercom
  - Sprinkler system
  - Water flow detector
  - Fire detection and alarm system
  - Automatic fire monitoring system connected to the fire station
- Must be separated from other parts of the building by a compartment wall/floor with at least **2-hour fire resistance period**
- Must be readily accessible, preferably directly from the open air
- Route to fire command centre shall be protected

> **Citation:** By-law 238, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 239: Voice Communication System

Two separate approved continuously electrically supervised voice communication systems required:

- **(a) Fireman intercom** — provided in:
  - Every fire fighting access lobby or adjacent to fire fighting staircase
  - Refuge area, lift motor room, fire pump room, generator room, fire command centre
  - In accordance with the Tenth Schedule
- **(b) Public address system** — provided in accordance with the Tenth Schedule

> **Citation:** By-law 239, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 240: Electrical Isolation Switch

- Any building with floor area exceeding **1,000 sq m** per floor shall have an electrical isolation switch complying with **IEC 60947-3**
- Purpose: permit disconnection of electrical power supply to the relevant floor
- Location: fire fighting access lobby, adjacent to fire fighting staircase, or at exit door

> **Citation:** By-law 240, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 241: Special Requirements for Fire Alarm Systems

- Places with deaf persons or where audible alarm is undesirable shall have special fire alarm systems in accordance with **MS 1745**

> **Citation:** By-law 241, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 243: Fire Lift

- Fire lift required where topmost occupied floor is over **18 metres** above or basement storey is more than **9 metres** below fire appliance access level
- Penthouse occupying ≤50% of area of floor immediately below is exempted from this measurement
- Fire lift shall be in a separate protected shaft if it opens into a separate lobby
- Rate: one fire lift per group of lifts discharging into the fire fighting access lobby

> **Citation:** By-law 243, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-law 243A: Emergency Mode of Operation (Mains Power Failure)

- On mains power failure: all lifts return in sequence to designated floor (fire lift first), without answering calls, and park with doors open
- After parking: all lifts on emergency power resume normal operation
- If sufficient emergency power is available for all lifts, sequential parking is not required

> **Citation:** By-law 243A, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 243B: Fire Mode of Operation

- Initiated by signal from fire alarm panel (automatic or manual)
- All lifts return in sequence to designated floor (fire lift first), without answering calls
- Fire lift then available for fire brigade use via fireman's switch
- Fire lift only responds to car calls (not landing calls) in fire mode
- On mains power failure: operates under emergency power with same procedures
- Fireman intercom system required in lift car for communication between lift operator and fire command centre

> **Citation:** By-law 243B, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-law 245: Approval of Director General

- Any fire fighting equipment or fire safety installation other than those conforming to Parts VII and VIII must be submitted to and approved by the Director General before commencement of work
- Plans, drawings and calculations of all fire fighting installations shall be submitted to the Fire and Rescue Department

> **Citation:** By-law 245, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 248: Markings on Wet Riser, etc.

- Wet riser, dry riser, sprinkler and other fire installation pipes/fittings: identified in **red colour**
- All cabinets and recessed areas for fire installations/extinguishers: clearly identified to the satisfaction of the Director General

> **Citation:** By-law 248, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 249: Smoke Control

Smoke control system (natural or mechanical) in accordance with **MS 1780** required where:

- **(a)** Compartmentation relates to atrium conditions (By-law 252A)
- **(b)** Any compartment exceeds **2,000 sq m**
- **(c)** Any basement where total area exceeds **1,000 sq m**, except:
  - **(i)** Basement car park compartmented from rest — requires smoke purging system
  - **(ii)** Plant/equipment room ≤**250 sq m** compartmented — two remotely located doors required; single door permitted if most remote part <**8 metres** from door
  - **(iii)** Plant/equipment room >**250 sq m** but ≤**1,000 sq m** — smoke vent or smoke purging at least **10 air changes per hour**
  - **(iv)** Service area (storeroom/workshop, staff only) compartmented — smoke venting or purging at **10 air changes per hour** in lieu of engineered system

### Basement Smoke Vents

- Required if total aggregate basement floor area exceeds **200 sq m** but does not exceed **1,000 sq m**
- In lieu of smoke vent: smoke purging system or engineered smoke control system for car park or other occupancy

> **Citation:** By-law 249, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2021)

## By-law 252A: Atriums in Buildings

An atrium is permitted provided:

- **(a)** Horizontal dimension ≥**6 metres** and opening area ≥**95 sq m**
- **(b)** Exit separately enclosed from atrium (exit access may be within atrium)
- **(c)** Atrium is open and unobstructed
- **(d)** Building fully protected by automatic sprinkler system
- **(e)** Sprinkler may be omitted for atrium ceiling if >**17 metres** above floor; for ceiling height >17m, water monitor, deluge or extended-throw sprinkler required for entire atrium space
- **(f)** Smoke control/exhaust system per Table 1:

| Atrium Height (m) | Volume (m³) | Smoke Exhaust | Air Change/hr | Air Supply |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ≤17 | ≤17,000 | 19 m³/s | 6 | Gravity, 75% of exhaust |
| ≤17 | >17,000 | 19 m³/s | 4 | Gravity, 17% of exhaust |
| >17 | any | — | 4 | Mechanical, 75% of exhaust |

- **(g)** Smoke control activated by: smoke detector at top of atrium, sprinkler system, automatic detector (not manual break glass), and manual control accessible to Fire and Rescue Department
- **(h)** Atrium separated from adjacent space by **1-hour fire resistance** fire barrier, except:
  - Up to 3 levels may open directly without enclosure
  - Glass wall may substitute if sprinklers spaced ≤**1.8m** apart on both sides, ≤**0.3m** from glass; glass must be tempered, wired or laminated

> **Citation:** By-law 252A, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)

## By-law 253: Emergency Power System

- Emergency power shall provide illumination and power automatically within **60 seconds** of failure
- Must power: smoke control, illumination, fire alarm, fire pumps, public address, fire lifts, and other emergency systems
- Must have capacity for simultaneous operation of **all fire lifts and one other lift**
- All emergency wiring: metal conduit or fire-resisting mineral insulated cables, laid along areas of least fire risk
- Supply types:
  - **(a) Storage battery**: capacity for at least **1½ hours** at not less than 87½% system voltage
  - **(b) Generator set**: sufficient capacity with automatic starting on failure of normal supply

> **Citation:** By-law 253, UBBL 1984

## By-law 253A: Emergency Lighting

- Emergency lighting shall provide sufficient illumination for escape purposes
- Type: self-contained or supplied from emergency power system or central battery bank
- Must comply with **MS 619** and **MS 2687**
- Duration: not less than **3 hours** in event of normal supply failure

> **Citation:** By-law 253A, UBBL 1984 (Amendment 2012/2021)
