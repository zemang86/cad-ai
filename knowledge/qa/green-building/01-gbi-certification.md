# Green Building Index (GBI) Certification

## Overview

Established in **2009** by PAM (Persatuan Arkitek Malaysia) and ACEM (Association of Consulting Engineers Malaysia).

## Six Assessment Criteria

1. **Energy Efficiency**
2. **Indoor Environmental Quality**
3. **Sustainable Site Planning & Management**
4. **Materials & Resources**
5. **Water Efficiency**
6. **Innovation**

> **Citation**: Green Building Index Malaysia

## Rating Tiers

| Rating | Minimum Points |
|--------|---------------|
| **Certified** | **50 points** |
| Silver | 66 points |
| Gold | 76 points |
| Platinum | 86 points |

- Separate scorecards for **residential**, **commercial**, and **industrial** developments
- Maximum possible score: 100 points

> **Citation**: Green Building Index Malaysia

## Certification Process

1. Application submission
2. GBI certifier appointment
3. Design assessment (DA)
4. Provisional GBI certification
5. Completion and Verification Assessment (CVA)
6. Final GBI award

- Reassessment required **every 3 years**

> **Citation**: Green Building Index Malaysia

## Sustainable Timber

- **50%+** of wood-based materials must be **FSC** (Forest Stewardship Council) or **MTCC** (Malaysian Timber Certification Council) certified

> **Citation**: Green Building Index Malaysia, Materials & Resources criteria
